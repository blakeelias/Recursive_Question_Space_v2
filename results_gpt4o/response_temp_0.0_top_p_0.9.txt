Justified True Belief:
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they have justification for believing the proposition
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary for the proposition to be true
- For a person to know a proposition, it is sufficient that they believe the proposition, have justification for believing the proposition, and the proposition is true

Reliabilism:
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief in the proposition is produced by a reliable cognitive process
- A reliable cognitive process is one that produces a high proportion of true beliefs over false ones

No False Lemmas:

Causal Theory of Knowledge:
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that there is an appropriate causal connection between the fact that makes the proposition true and the person's belief in the proposition

Virtue Epistemology:
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that they believe the proposition
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief is the result of exercising intellectual virtue
- An intellectual virtue is a trait that reliably leads to the acquisition of true beliefs

Coherentism:

- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true

- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true

Externalism:

- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true

Internalism:

Truth-Tracking Theory:

- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that the proposition is true
- For a person to know a proposition, it is necessary that their belief tracks the truth of the proposition
- A belief tracks the truth if, in nearby possible worlds, the person would not believe the proposition if it were false and would believe it if it were true
